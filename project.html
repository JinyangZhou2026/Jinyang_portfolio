Overview

Product concept and final direction.

Research

The project responds to early-childhood wellbeing, attention, and emotional development.

Primary and secondary users define the behavioral, educational, and caregiving context.

Benchmarking identifies a gap between open-ended play and guided gratification.

Journey

The user flow translates therapy logic into repeatable physical interactions.

Three use modes extend the product from focused play to listening and shelf display.

Components

The system combines a perforated board, musical keys, story cards, batteries, and a detachable narrative module.

Narrative Module

The narrative module manages story sequence, feedback, display, volume, and detachable listening.

Interaction System

Physical interaction is built through musical keys, tactile pegs, and a foldable perforated panel.

CMF & Prototype

Material choices, dimensions, and study models support manufacturability and daily use.

Market

The market proposal positions Melody Adventurer between accessible learning products and premium therapeutic play.
