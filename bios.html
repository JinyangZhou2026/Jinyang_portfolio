Context & Brief

Research defines the classroom context, water-access challenges, usability needs, safety requirements, and local constraints.

Filtration Principle

The concept translates biosand filtration into a legible product system that teachers and children can understand and maintain.

Ergonomics & Form

Dimensional studies, anthropometry, and form exploration translate the system into a stable object for a floating classroom.

System Overview

The product architecture connects filtration, pumping, dispensing, cleaning, and positioning into one integrated water station.

Pump System

The vacuum pump is communicated as a mechanical subsystem through components, interaction sequence, and detail renderings.

Diffuser, Body & Water Level

Internal components organize sand separation, water distribution, structural support, and visible filter-state feedback.

Faucet & Folding Basin

Water delivery, handwashing, transparent inspection, and soap storage are organized around compact classroom routines.

Installation & Use

Placement, anchoring, and storyboard pages show how the product fits into the floating school and daily use routines.

Maintenance & Education

Maintenance, packaging, assembly, and classroom workshop material extend the product into an educational service system.

Circularity & Scaling

The closing section connects material reuse, recycling policy, potable water impact, market position, and possible expansion contexts.
