Hero Project 02 Framework / Research-led Product Design

Insight to Product Concept

Use this page to show how research evidence becomes design criteria, form decisions, interaction logic, prototypes, and a coherent physical product concept.

Framework only

No real project, product claim, research result, or outcome is assumed. Replace every prompt with verified material from the selected project.

01 / Context & Design Question

Introduce one focused human need—not the entire research archive.

Context & Audience

Add the user, situation, and reason this product opportunity matters.

Give recruiters only the context required to understand the design challenge. Identify the user and environment without turning the opening into a long academic report.

Design Question

Frame a precise “How might we…” question that the product can answer.

Keep the question narrow enough to evaluate the final concept. Avoid promising social or behavioral impact that the project did not verify.

02 / Research to Design Criteria

Make the bridge from evidence to design decisions explicit.

Evidence 01

Add one verified user or contextual finding.

Source: add interview, observation, literature, or benchmark evidence.

→ Add the design criterion it created
Evidence 02

Add the second finding that changed the product direction.

Show why it matters rather than displaying every research method.

→ Add the resulting product requirement
Evidence 03

Add one constraint, tension, or unmet need.

Use a visual excerpt, diagram, or concise quotation where appropriate.

→ Add the resulting interaction or form principle

03 / Sketch Exploration

Show several genuinely different directions before revealing the chosen one.

Direction A

Add a distinct form or interaction approach.

Include selected sketches and short annotations about its opportunity and limitation.

Direction B

Add a meaningfully different proposal.

Show variation in product architecture, user interaction, form language, or use scenario.

Direction C

Add the direction that leads toward the final concept.

Do not present every sketch—select the drawings that reveal your design judgement.

06 / Product Resolution

Architecture

CMF & Construction

Final Visual 01

08 / Contribution & Current Status

Separate personal ownership, verified learning, and untested assumptions.

Personal Contribution

Add exactly what you researched, designed, modelled, prototyped, and visualized.

If this was team work, name the collaborators’ responsibilities and avoid presenting shared work as individual ownership.

Current Status & Next Step

State what was tested, what remains conceptual, and what you would validate next.

Do not invent implementation or impact. Mature reflection is stronger than an unsupported claim of resolution.
