Hero Project 03 Framework / Commercial Product Storytelling

Consumer Product Launch Visualization

Use this page to demonstrate how a product proposition becomes an emotionally engaging, commercially coherent launch system across CGI, motion, and campaign formats.

Framework only

No product category, brand, audience, feature, or campaign outcome is assumed. Use a real brief or clearly label the future project as self-initiated.

01 / Commercial Brief

Define what the product offers, who should care, and where the campaign will appear.

Product & Proposition

Add the consumer product and its single most important value proposition.

Choose a product with meaningful form, CMF, interaction, or function so the project still supports an industrial and product design identity.

Audience & Channels

Add the target customer, buying context, launch moment, and required formats.

Specify where the visual system must work: product page, social media, retail display, launch film, presentation, or digital advertising.

02 / Visual Strategy & Art Direction

Turn the product proposition into a deliberate visual language.

Moodboard / Direction

Add references for tone, color, material, lighting, composition, and motion.

Do not show a decorative moodboard alone. Annotate how the direction expresses the product promise and separates the product from competitors.

Lighting & Color

Add controlled visual tests.

Compare directions and explain the selected emotional and material effect.

Composition & Type

Add the graphic system around the CGI.

Show framing, scale, hierarchy, copy placement, and brand consistency.

03 / Storyboard & Motion Structure

Plan a short product story—not a sequence of unrelated effects.

  1. 01 / Attract

    Visual Hook

    Add the opening image or motion that establishes tone and earns attention.

  2. 02 / Reveal

    Product Introduction

    Add how the full product is revealed with clear silhouette and material quality.

  3. 03 / Explain

    Feature Moment

    Add one benefit-led sequence that connects function to customer value.

  4. 04 / Desire

    Detail & Experience

    Add tactile detail, use context, CMF, or emotional imagery.

  5. 05 / Close

    Brand & Action

    Add the final product promise, brand moment, or launch call to action.

04 / CGI Development

Show the craft behind believable material, lighting, and product presence.

Model Preparation

Add CAD cleanup, topology, detailing, or asset creation.

Clarify whether the model was designed, supplied, optimized, or rebuilt.

Evidence: wireframe or before / after
Material & CMF

Add shader development and surface references.

Connect finish decisions to brand tone and product positioning.

Evidence: material test sheet
Lighting & Camera

Add lighting studies and camera language.

Explain how scale, edge definition, reflections, and focus guide attention.

Evidence: selected tests

05 / Feature Communication

Use one controlled sequence to connect function with consumer benefit.

Feature Film Placeholder / 6–12 Seconds

Add one feature or use moment worth animating.

Begin with the customer benefit, then decide whether assembly, transparency, transformation, particles, or close-up motion is actually necessary to explain it.

Benefit → Visual Device → Customer Understanding

06 / Hero Launch Film

Reserve the strongest motion outcome for a concise 15–30 second launch film.

08 / Contribution & Delivery

Close with production ownership, technical specifications, and honest project status.

Personal Contribution

Add ownership across art direction, modelling, rendering, animation, editing, and graphic composition.

Credit any supplied model, music, typeface, brand asset, collaborator, or external production resource.

Delivery & Status

Add channel formats, aspect ratios, resolution, duration, and delivery package.
